# Content Foundations

## Quick reference

- US English spelling
- Use contractions (it's, you're, don't, can't)
- Sentence case everywhere
- Active voice preferred
- Address user directly (you/your)
- Oxford comma in lists
- No exclamation marks
- Numerals for 10+, spell out 0–9
- Dates: October 25, 2025 (Oct 25, 2025 if space-limited)
- Time: 12-hour with am/pm, space before suffix (3:00 pm)

## Grammar and mechanics

### Spelling
US English. Check [Merriam-Webster](https://www.merriam-webster.com/) when unsure.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| analyze | analyse |

### Contractions
Use contractions for a human tone. Exception: legal terms or intentional emphasis.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| We'll refund any remaining credits | We will refund any remaining credits |

### Oxford comma
Always use in lists of three or more.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Invite participants, collect responses, and share insights | Invite participants, collect responses and share insights |

### Dashes
- **Em dash (—):** Sparingly, with spaces on either side. Prefer colons, commas, or better sentence structure.
- **En dash (–):** For ranges, no spaces.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| All set — your study is ready | All set—your study is ready |
| 5–10 participants | 5 - 10 participants |

### Periods
Use only if a block contains two or more full sentences. Skip in labels, tooltips, list items, short descriptions.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Invite participants to your study | Invite participants to your study. |
| Get started | Get started. |

### Exclamation marks
Never use in product copy. Avoid casual interjections like "Oops."

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Your study is ready to launch | Your study is ready to launch! |

### Ellipses
Only for truncated text or loading states. Never for dramatic pause.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Analyzing your data… | Something went wrong… |

## Capitalization

Sentence case everywhere — headers, labels, CTAs.

### Branded features (capitalize)
Reach, Clips, Reels, In-Product Prompts, Unmoderated, Interview Studies

### Artifacts (lowercase)
study, studies, reel, reels, task, block, project, campaign, prompt, recording

**Exception:** "Clips" stays capitalized even as an entity due to legacy naming.

| Feature | Entities |
|---------|----------|
| Unmoderated | unmoderated study, blocks |
| Clips | Clips recording, Clips recordings |
| Reels | reel, reels |
| Reach | campaign, email |

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Explore the Enterprise plan | Explore the Enterprise Plan |
| Create a study | Create a Study |
| Recruit from the panel | Recruit from the Panel |

## Numbers

- Spell out 0–9
- Use numerals for 10+
- Use numerals for percentages (5%)
- Use symbols: $, %, +, –
- Abbreviate large numbers: 2M+, 1.5K

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| three tasks | 3 tasks |
| 15 participants | fifteen participants |
| Target 2M+ participants | Target more than 2 million participants |

### Dates
Month Day, Year format. Spell out month (abbreviate if space-limited). Never use slashes.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| October 27, 2025 | 27/10/2025 |
| Oct 27, 2025 | 10/27/2025 |

### Time
12-hour clock, no periods in am/pm, space between number and suffix.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| 6 pm, 11:35 am | 18:00, 6pm, 6 p.m. |
| 12 noon | 12:00 pm |
| 9 am–12 pm | 9am - 12pm |

## Terminology

### Preferred terms
- **participant** (not tester)
- **task** (not mission)
- **unmoderated study** (not maze)
- **moderated study** (not interview study)

### Spelling
| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| email | e-mail |
| login (noun), log in (verb) | log-in |
| signup (noun), sign up (verb) | sign-up |

## Accessibility

### Reading level
Aim for Grade 6 or lower. Critical for participant-facing copy.

### Device-agnostic language
Use "select," "choose," "open" — not "click" or "tap."

### No directional language
Avoid "on the left," "above," "scroll down." Refer to labels or sections instead.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Open each screen to see how participants interacted | Click the screens below to see how participants interacted |

### No sensory language
Avoid "see," "hear," "look at." Focus on actions or content.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| You'll find more information in the next section | See below for more information |

### Descriptive link text
Never use "click here" or "learn more" alone.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Learn more about sharing your study | Click here to learn more |
| Learn more about AI follow-ups | Learn more |

### Error messages
Always explain what happened + how to fix it.

## Style

### Active voice
Prefer active voice. Use passive only to de-emphasize requirements.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Find Clips in the Results page | Clips can be found in the Results page |
| Microphone access is required to participate | We require microphone access to participate |

### Second person
Address the user as "you." Minimize "we/our."

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| You'll receive a download link via email | We'll send you a download link via email |

### Formatting
- **Bold:** Only for UI element references. Never for emphasis.
- **Italics:** Avoid — hard to read on screen.
- **Line breaks:** Use to separate distinct ideas.
- **Emoji:** Never use in UI.

## Filler words to remove

- "You can"
- "It's possible to"
- "Seem to be"
- "Start to"
- "Try to"
- "Just"
- "Really"
- "Easily"

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Share your study with participants | You can start sharing your study with participants |
